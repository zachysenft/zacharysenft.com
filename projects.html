Virtual Clue-Less

Clue-Less was a project I accomplished with four others in response to the Covid-19 pandemic. We were tasked with developing a game that allows players to play across a network, with subcomponents of a database, game-board interface, server interface, and client interface. I played a key role in the group as the lead programmer. My main duties consisted of subsystem design and game logic development in our specified language (Java). I also oversaw the database connection as I am well-versed in SQL and assisted developing the interfaces using the Java Swing framework.

This project was especially challenging as the group was tasked with starting the project without any set rules or guidance in place. As a result, the team chose to follow an agile development life cycle and worked in sprints through our four defined periods. In our first phase, we defined our subsystems and developed a document detailing the precise requirements for the overall application. Additionally, we collaborated on UML class diagrams to outline the classes and back-room functionality that the game requires to properly run. Once this was completed, we were able to develop our skeletal system with the main components and ensure that they were connected. Next was the base game logic development, where I took the lead and oversaw the integration between our back-end code and our front-end interfaces. Finally, we collaborated by designing each interface such that it properly reflected what is going on in the game and was connected via network. Each increment was accompanied by a demo that outlined what we accomplished during the sprint and what our goals are for the upcoming increments. This project was extremely valuable in further developing my teamwork and collaboration skills in a professional, software engineering environment alongside the soft skills it takes to be a good team member.

This project showcases my technical abilities, my strength interacting with others in a professional environment, and my management skills as I took on a lead role in the development. Both technical and soft skills I learned in this experience are what I am looking forward to nurturing at my future position and am excited to build upon! Positive experiences such as this one are what further motivates me and reminds me that I am on the right path to do what I want in my career.
